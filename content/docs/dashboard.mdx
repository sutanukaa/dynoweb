## Top Of Page

This is the main dashboard entry area.

It tells users they are on the store performance overview, shows whether tracking is active, and surfaces quick wins if the app has already found suggestions.

<img src="/topOfPage.png" alt="Dashboard top of page" />

## Key Metrics Row

This row gives the fastest high-level summary of store activity.

It includes attributed revenue, total tracked events, page views, and form signals, so users can quickly understand traffic volume and engagement quality.

<img src="/overview.png" alt="Dashboard key metrics row" />

## Activity Overview Row

This row helps users understand how traffic is behaving over time and where it is coming from.

The left chart shows the daily activity trend, and the right chart shows the device breakdown across desktop, tablet, and mobile.

<img src="/activity.png" alt="Dashboard activity overview row" />

## Behavior Signals Row

This row explains what kind of data DynoWeb is capturing and which signals matter most right now.

The left chart shows the mix of clicks, scrolls, forms, and page views, while the right chart highlights the strongest signals such as scroll depth, rage clicks, dead clicks, and other behavior indicators.

<img src="/behavior.png" alt="Dashboard behavior signals row" />

## Most Problematic Pages

This table highlights pages that may need attention first.

It surfaces pages with stronger frustration or error signals, and pages where users are not scrolling deeply enough.

<img src="/problematic.png" alt="Dashboard most problematic pages table" />

## Most Interacted Pages

This table highlights the pages receiving the most engagement, mainly based on clicks and views.

It helps users quickly jump into the pages that matter most.

<img src="/interacted.png" alt="Dashboard most interacted pages table" />
